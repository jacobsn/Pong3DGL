Summary

Our game is a first-person OpenGL remake of the classic video game Pong. Pong3D features advanced lighting, shadows, and networked game play. This is not your father's arcade game.

In Pong3D players are assigned a wall to defend. They must use the paddle to prevent the ball from traveling past their wall. You win the networked game if you can bounce the ball past your opponents wall.

In addition to networked play, we included some features to make it fun to play without an opponent. For times when you can't find anyone to play against we built Speed and Target mode. In Speed mode, you rack up points by trying to keep the ball from hitting your wall as its speed slowly increases. In Target mode, the goal is to use our vector controller to aim your ball at the targets.

Description

Network Play

Allows for competitive game play via the Internet. One machine acts as a host and the other acts as a client. The host machine is responsible for calculating all physics and sending the updated information to the client. The client sends its paddle positions to the host so that correct collisions can be detected. To make it even more fun, both players can see where the other's paddle is moving.

Local Play

  • Target Mode : Use the vector controller to aim your ball at the targets. Each time you hit a target the next one is worth more points. If you miss the target the points/target return to startup level. To add to the excitement, the ball speeds up if you don't hit the targets.
  • Speed Mode : The ball slowly speeds up... ok?

User-Interface

The Pong3D user-interface is intentionally simple and elegant.

  • Mouse: The position of your mouse on the screen determines your paddle location. If you use the right mouse button you can select which mode you want to play. During target mode, after you hit the ball and before it hits the back wall, clicking the left mouse button (LMB) will allow you to control the gravity. The gravity is determined by the distance and direction of the mouse from the center of the window (hint: don't use too much gravity). The gravity is applied as long as you hold down the LMB.
  • Spacebar: In network-host, speed, and target modes hitting the space bar will either start or pause the game.
